UX & Web Designer

LAURA MALIQI

Questions about whether design is necessary or affordable are quite beside the point: design is inevitable. The alternative to good design is bad design, not no design at all. Douglas Martin

I am a postgraduate student at the Kingston University completing the User Experience (UX) Designer Master’s course and a BA graduate in Economics (Doba Faculty of Applied Business and Social Sciences, Slovenia, 2010).

Communication comes naturally to me and I believe this is important skill to possess. The next skill on the list would be my desire to understand things and getting to the bottom of things. And the third my affinity for creativity. The list is longer, but let’s say that these three are paramount when trying to gain an understanding of who my users are and what are their needs, understanding what are the issues they’re facing and when I’m envisioning a better design solution for them

As a long time user of websites and mobile app’s who is now taking a viewpoint of a UX designer, it makes me thrilled to think I’ll be able to create, design and build interfaces, services and products that will be both usable and useful, a fun and a beautiful experience both for the user and for their designer.

So, as I embark deeper into the depth of the UX domain I will be putting all my effort in enhancing the experience by focusing on usability, accessibility, credibility and let us not forget findability – as Peter Morville says:
“Findability precedes usability. In the alphabet and on the Web. You can’t use what you can’t find.”
